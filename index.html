MathWOOT (AoPS)
Math Worldwide Online Olympiad Training · year-round · Sept–March · $895/year
The two levels — very different entry points
Level 1
Bridge from AIME to olympiad proof-writing. Covers all four olympiad subjects. Builds proof-writing fluency and contest-specific skills to qualify for USAJMO/USAMO.
Prerequisite: AIME score 5–9 + all AoPS Intro & Intermediate courses done. 22 classes, Sept–March.
Level 2
For olympiad qualifiers targeting best performance at USAMO/IMO. Alternates content between two versions — can be taken two consecutive years without repeating material.
Prerequisite: MathWOOT L1 completed OR AIME score 9+. Same schedule and cost.

★
76 IMO medals since 2015 — 58 gold, 15 silver, 3 bronze — the strongest track record of any program
★
Direct solution feedback from instructors — not just answer checking
★
MOP participants get it free — a direct signal of its alignment with the US olympiad pipeline
→
3 practice AIME exams (L1) + 3 USA(J)MO / IMO practices (L2) — real contest simulation built in
Honest limitations
→
Hard prerequisite gate: AIME 5–9 for Level 1. No point trying to enter early — the material assumes that ability level

O
OTIS (Olympiad Training for Individual Study)
Evan Chen · proof-based · year-round · need-blind financial aid · no age restriction
Three difficulty tracks
B-level (beginner)
Entry-level olympiad proof work. Covers standard topics with beginner-friendly problems. Designed for students just crossing into proof-based work from AIME-level material.
D-level (intermediate)
Mid-range olympiad material. For students who can read proofs fluently and are targeting USAJMO qualification or first USAMO qualification.
Z-level (hardest)
Hardest track. For MOP-level students targeting IMO. Evan Chen's highest-difficulty handouts — the material used to train future IMO team members.
What makes OTIS different from MathWOOT
★
Run entirely by Evan Chen — the most respected name in US olympiad training. His EGMO, OTIS Excerpts, and handouts are the canonical self-study resources.
★
No age restriction, no grade requirement — admission is purely about mathematical readiness and proof ability
★
Need-blind, full financial aid available — explicitly accessible regardless of income
★
Individual, self-paced handout model — very different from MathWOOT's structured class format. More like a curated problem curriculum with mentor feedback.
→
Admission is not merit-based competition — it's about proof readiness and English ability. Much more accessible than programs with hard score cutoffs.
OTIS vs MathWOOT — the key distinction
→
MathWOOT = structured class with live instruction, homework, exams, and direct instructor feedback on a fixed schedule
→
OTIS = curated problem sets and handouts with periodic feedback; more flexible pacing; Evan Chen personally reviews applications and direction
Many top students do both simultaneously — MathWOOT for structured instruction, OTIS for deep problem practice.

The four levels — mapped to competition stage
Level 1
AMC 10/12 midrange → AIME with 1–3 correct. Entry point for strong AMC students approaching AIME.
Level 2
AIME with 4–11 correct. The main development zone for most serious AIME competitors.
Level 3
AIME with 12+ correct → Olympiad level. Pre-USAMO territory.
Level 4
Full olympiad. USAMO/IMO territory. USA(J)MO qualifiers auto-admitted.
Distinctive features

Subject-specific courses — can take Geometry at Level 2 and Combinatorics at Level 3 simultaneously, for example. Very flexible level mixing by subject.
★
Intense daily engagement (2.5 hrs/day, 5 days/week) — more immersive than year-round programs
