Can MathWOOT and OTIS run simultaneously? Yes — here's why
✓
They solve different problems
MathWOOT: structured instruction with a cohort, fixed schedule, exam simulations, direct feedback on specific solutions. OTIS: self-paced problem sets, individual pace, Evan Chen's curated handouts. One is the class; the other is the practice.
✓
OTIS is genuinely self-paced — it flexes around MathWOOT's schedule
During MathWOOT exam weeks and AIME/USAMO season: reduce OTIS to near zero. During quieter stretches: ramp OTIS back up. This is exactly how top students manage both.
✓
Many of the top US olympiad students run both simultaneously in grades 8–11
This is the standard configuration among USAMO/MOP-level students — it's not unusual or exceptional.
→
The burnout risk is not the programs themselves — it's over-scheduling everything at once
AwesomeMath Summer is intensive (2.5 hrs/day for 3 weeks) but contained. It doesn't run during the school year. The yearly rhythm: school year = MathWOOT + OTIS; summer = AwesomeMath + residential program. No overlap.
The one guard against burnout at every stage: weekends fully free, one non-math interest protected (music, sport, anything), and the student retains genuine ownership of his trajectory — he chooses what he works on each day within the structure, not a parent-imposed hourly schedule. The huaren.us community and competition math coaches are unanimous: the students who sustain this intensity for 6+ years are the ones who feel math is something they do, not something done to them.
